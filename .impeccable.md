## Design Context

### Users
Players are friends testing a browser-based, Metin-inspired block action RPG, often in short duo sessions. They want fast readability, satisfying combat feedback, visible character identity, and reasons to coordinate instead of simply hitting the same target.

### Brand Personality
Blocky, punchy, adventurous. The game should feel like a compact arena RPG prototype with toy-like immediacy, clear combat roles, and readable loot dopamine.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the browser/Canvas prototype lightweight, but push the presentation toward a sharper tabletop-arena look: earthy battleground colors, class-coded silhouettes, crisp HUD panels, chunky skill buttons, and strong hit/loot effects. Avoid generic neon dark UI, stock fantasy chrome, and overcomplicated 3D unless the game deliberately moves to a new engine.

### Design Principles
1. Make class identity visible in the character, HUD, skills, and multiplayer presence.
2. Favor duo mechanics where one player sets up a state and the other detonates it.
3. Keep systems data-driven so new classes, items, mobs, and skills can be added without editing the whole game loop.
4. Improve visual quality through sprites, motion, lighting, and feedback before adding heavy tech.
5. Preserve the prototype's quick browser start while preparing the codebase for a future Vite/Phaser step.
